## Team 01 — Impact

**Team Dafne** 

[GitHub](https://github.com/okostec-events/nido_hack_26_team-01)

Impact helps companies and municipalities show — not just claim — that their work is making a difference. ## Team 04 — MatchUp

**Team Tholeani**

[GitHub](https://github.com/okostec-events/nido_hack_26_team-04)

MatchUp connects high school students with internships, competitions, and hackathons that actually fit them. Students fill in a profile with their skills and achievements, and the platform uses AI to score how well each opportunity matches — so students spend less time scrolling through listings that aren't right for them and more time applying to ones that are.

## Team 05 — Student Agenda

**Team Craighouse Techs** 

[GitHub](https://github.com/okostec-events/nido_hack_26_team-05)

A student planner built around a problem the team members know personally: phones get taken away at school, but you still need somewhere to track homework, deadlines, and announcements. The app runs in any browser, lets students customize it to their schedule, and can import and export data as a spreadsheet so nothing gets locked in.

## Team 06 — Hobbyist

[GitHub](https://github.com/okostec-events/nido_hack_26_team-06)

Hobbyist helps people swap out addictive habits by finding hobbies that actually fit their life — their schedule, budget, and physical situation. It walks you through a short questionnaire to figure out what you want to change and why, then recommends concrete activities and shows you how to get started, where to go, and how to connect with others doing the same thing. The progress system is designed to give you the same sense of reward you were getting from the habit you're trying to leave behind.

## Team 07 — Brujula - Chilean Immigrant & Tourist Onboarding Guide



[GitHub](https://github.com/okostec-events/nido_hack_26_team-07)

Arriving in Chile means navigating a tangle of bureaucratic steps — legal registration, banking, housing, health services — often without knowing what order they need to happen in or what a mistake will cost you. This app guides immigrants and tourists through the process one step at a time, organizing everything into a clear flow so nothing falls through the cracks.

## Team 08 — PassionPath

[GitHub](https://github.com/okostec-events/nido_hack_26_team-08)

PassionPath helps people in Santiago find volunteer work and community projects that match what they care about and what they can actually commit to. You tell it where you live, what causes interest you, and how much budget you have, and it shows you nearby NGOs and initiatives with a score for how good a fit each one is — no more digging through websites trying to figure out if something is right for you.

## Team 09 — GridScope

[GitHub](https://github.com/okostec-events/nido_hack_26_team-09)

GridScope is an interactive sandbox for planning renewable energy projects. Using a Simcity like interface, it walks users through the full process of scouting potential sites, designing the installation, and generating an impact report. It's aimed at anyone who needs to evaluate where and how to add solar or wind capacity without hiring a consultant for every early-stage question.
